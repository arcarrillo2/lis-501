Interpreting the LDA Topic Visualizations (pyLDAvis)

The visualizations below show the topics learned by Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) for scientific and informal nutrition articles. Each visualization consists of two coordinated panels.

Intertopic Distance Map (Left Panel)

  • Each circle represents one topic.
  • Circle size reflects how prevalent the topic is in the corpus.
  • Distance between circles indicates how different topics are based on vocabulary.
  • Overlapping or nearby topics share similar language.

Top Terms Bar Chart (Right Panel)

  • Shows the most important words for the selected topic.
  • Blue bars indicate overall word frequency.
  • Red bars show how strongly a word is associated with the selected topic.

How to Use the Visualization

  • Click on a topic bubble to explore its defining words.
  • Adjust the λ slider:
    • λ = 1.0 highlights the most frequent words.
    • Lower λ (≈ 0.4) highlights words unique to a topic.

Together, these visualizations allow comparison of how nutrition topics are structured and emphasized in scientific research versus informal nutrition writing.
